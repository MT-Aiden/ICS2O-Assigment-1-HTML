Life of Christopher Adams

History

Christopher Evans was born in Aberdovey and raised in Wales before attending Christ College, Brecon (1941–49).
He served in the RAF for two years (1950–52), then worked as a scientific journalist and writer until 1957, when he enrolled in a B.A. study in Psychology at University College, London,
where he graduated with honours in 1960. Following a summer scholarship at Duke University in the United States, where he met his American wife, Nancy Fullmer, he accepted a
position as a Research Assistant in the Physics Laboratory at the University of Reading, where he worked on eye movements with Professor R. W. Ditchburn. He proceeded to the
Division of Computer Science, National Physical Laboratory, Teddington, after getting his PhD (the title of his thesis was "Pattern Perception and the Stabilised Retinal Image").

Contributions

In 1979, Christopher Evans published The Mighty Micro: The Impact of the Computer Revolution, a book on the impending microcomputer revolution that featured forecasts for
the future up to the year 2000. This book was also
published in the United States as The Micro Millennium (New York: The Viking Press, ISBN 0-670-47400-2). He later wrote and hosted a six-part television series
based on this book for ATV, which was shown posthumously by ITV between October and December 1979. Cults of Unreason, a study of Scientology and other pseudoscience, and Landscapes of the Night: How and Why We Dream are among
his other writings. Evans conducted a series of interviews with computer pioneers suc
h as Konrad Zuse and Grace Hopper in the 1970s.Christopher Evans
was also the editor of two collections of poetry.
